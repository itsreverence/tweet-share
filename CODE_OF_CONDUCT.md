# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

Contributors and maintainers should communicate respectfully, assume good faith, accept constructive feedback, and keep discussions focused on improving the project.

Harassment, discrimination, threats, sexualized attention, publication of another person's private information, and deliberate disruption are not acceptable. Do not post webhook URLs, account credentials, private messages, or private/protected social-media content.

## Scope and enforcement

This standard applies in repository issues, pull requests, reviews, and other project spaces. Maintainers may edit, hide, or remove contributions and may temporarily or permanently restrict participation when behavior is unsafe or persistently disruptive.

Use GitHub's reporting and blocking tools for platform abuse. For repository-specific concerns that can safely be discussed publicly, open an issue and ask to contact the maintainer privately without including sensitive details.

This policy is intentionally short for a small project and may be expanded if the contributor community grows.
